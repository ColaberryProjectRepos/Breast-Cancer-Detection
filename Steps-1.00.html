1.1 Background Information

Breast cancer is the most common malignancy among women, accounting for nearly 1 in 3 cancers diagnosed among women in the United States, and it is the second leading cause of cancer death among women. Breast Cancer occurs as a result of abnormal growth of cells in the breast tissue commonly referred to as a Tumor. A tumor does not mean cancer - tumors can be benign (not cancerous) and malignant (cancerous). Tests such as MRI, Mammogram, Ultrasound, and Biopsy are commonly used to diagnose Breast Cancer.
